What does define the structure of a group?

Distribution of tasks, power and resources.

The power of decision-making.

The perils of "structurelessness"

[...] way of masking power.

[...] awareness of power is limited to those who know the rules [of how decisions are made].
[...] groups which have not been deliberately structured in a particular manner [form] the basis for elites.

Are elites a bad thing?

Formal structures hinder elitism.

Networks of friends might compete for power

It's clear who is taking and who is giving power

People taking the power might be accountable

People giving the power might claim their interests

Women have suffered with invisible elitism for a long time

In any profession or organization these networks have created the "locker room" mentality and the "old school" ties which have effectively prevented women as a group [...] from having equal access to the sources of power or social reward.

Much of the energy of past women's movements has been directed to having the structures of decision-making and the selection processes formalized so that the exclusion of women could be confronted directly.

Elites don't need to be invisible.

Formal structures make explicit the criteria for deciding who shall exercise the power

Background, personality, allocation of time

Competence, dedication, talents, contributions

Having an established process for decision-making ensures that everyone can participate in it to some extent.

Informal structures may foster injustice

By forcing people onto roles they weren't chosen to play

E.g., the "star" women in the women's liberation movement

When an informal structure seem to work

  • Task oriented
  • Small and homogeneus
  • High degree of communication
  • Low degree of skill specialization
As long as friendship groups are the main means of organizational activity, elitism becomes institutionalized.

The lack of proper tasks distribution puts people against each other

The more unstructured a movement is, the less control it has over the directions in which it develops and the political actions in which it engages.
Some middle ground between domination and ineffectiveness can and must be found.

How to (formally?) structure a group?

Principles of democratic structuring

  • Delegation
  • Accountability
  • Distribution of authority
  • Rotation of tasks
  • Rational tasks allocation
  • Diffusion of information
  • Equal access to resources
When these principles are applied, [the] group will have the power to determine who shall exercise authority within it.
